Honor Code

Collaboration Policy

By enrolling in a course on ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME}, you are joining a special worldwide community of learners. The aspiration of ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} is to provide anyone in the world who has the motivation and ability to engage coursework from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Harvard University and the University of California, Berkeley the opportunity to attain the best MIT, Harvard and UC Berkeley-based educational experience that internet technology enables. You are part of the community who will help ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} achieve this goal.

${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} depends upon your motivation to learn the material and to do so with honesty. In order to participate in ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME}, you must agree to the Honor Code below and any additional terms specific to a class. This Honor Code, and any additional terms, will be posted on each class website.

${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} Honor Code Pledge

By enrolling in an ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} course, I agree that I will:

  • Complete all mid-terms and final exams with my own work and only my own work. I will not submit the work of any other person.
  • Maintain only one user account and not let anyone else use my username and/or password.
  • Not engage in any activity that would dishonestly improve my results, or improve or hurt the results of others.
  • Not post answers to problems that are being used to assess student performance.

Unless otherwise indicated by the instructor of an ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} course, learners on ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} are encouraged to:

  • Collaborate with others on the lecture videos, exercises, homework and labs.
  • Discuss with others general concepts and materials in each course.
  • Present ideas and written work to fellow ${settings.PLATFORM_NAME} learners or others for comment or criticism.
